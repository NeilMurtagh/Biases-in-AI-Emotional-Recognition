#Abstract
Algorithmic facial emotional recognition has been under scrutiny for some time. Its potential use cases reach from HR and online shopping to security or healthcare. But its potential pitfalls are equally manifold. This study investigates racial biases in a simple facial emotional recognition model trained by the FER-2013 dataset. Yet, we are not able to sustain our hypotheses that the model is less accurate for positively recognizing minorities or that there are discriminating patterns within the False Positives.
